Namasthe 🙏 to the Glorious Guru, Sri Âdinâtha (Śiva) , who instructed the knowledge of HATHA YOGA.

HaTha : Ha means sun and tha means moon symbolic of the twin energy forces which exist in everything.
Yoga : derived from sanskrit root yuj means to link or to connect,
yoga means to join or union

Hatha represents the forces of mind, and prana or vitality, which constitute the body and mind.
The moon is the mental energy of chitta. It is the subtle force which is concerned with the mental layers. The pranic force is like the sun, dynamic and active. The two create the extremities of introversion and extroversion. It is the practice of hatha yoga which enables the fluctuations between these two energies to become harmonious and unified into one force

Asana
“Sthirasukhamasanam’’
sthira = steady; sukham = comfortable; asanam = posture.
“That posture which is steady and comfortable is āsana.”

Asana has been derived from the root "ās‟ which means to sit. Generally the word "āsana" using in two contest, the body position which we adopt to sit and the object used for sitting.
